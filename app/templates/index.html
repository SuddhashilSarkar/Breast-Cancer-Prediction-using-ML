About the Project

Cancer Prediction Using Machine Learning is designed to enhance early breast cancer detection through advanced machine learning techniques. This project utilizes a Support Vector Machine (SVM) with a Radial Basis Function (RBF) kernel to accurately classify breast tumors as malignant or benign.
With an accuracy of 88%, this model analyzes clinical data to assist healthcare professionals in making informed decisions. Key features include high accuracy, clear visualizations of decision boundaries, and an intuitive user interface for easy predictions. This project represents a step forward in applying AI to improve healthcare outcomes.

Learn More About Cancer

Understanding Cancer

Cancer is a broad term for a group of diseases characterized by the uncontrolled growth and spread of abnormal cells. If the growth is not controlled, it can result in death. There are over 100 different types of cancer, each with its own characteristics, symptoms, and treatment options.

Common Types of Cancer

  • Breast Cancer: Affects the cells of the breast, commonly in women but also in men. Early detection through mammography is crucial.
  • Lung Cancer: Often associated with smoking, lung cancer is one of the leading causes of cancer-related deaths worldwide. Symptoms include coughing, chest pain, and shortness of breath.
  • Prostate Cancer: A common cancer in men, affecting the prostate gland. Screening involves a prostate-specific antigen (PSA) test.
  • Colorectal Cancer: Affects the colon or rectum. Regular screening, such as a colonoscopy, is recommended for early detection.
  • Skin Cancer: Includes melanoma and non-melanoma skin cancers. Prevention includes limiting exposure to UV radiation.

Symptoms to Watch For

While symptoms vary depending on the type of cancer, common signs include:

  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Persistent fatigue
  • Lumps or swelling in the body
  • Persistent cough or hoarseness
  • Changes in bowel or bladder habits
  • Unusual bleeding or discharge

It’s important to consult with a healthcare professional if you experience any of these symptoms.

Cancer Risk Factors

Some risk factors for cancer are modifiable, while others are not. Understanding these can help you take preventive measures:

  • Lifestyle Choices: Smoking, excessive alcohol consumption, poor diet, and lack of physical activity increase the risk of various cancers.
  • Environmental Exposure: Prolonged exposure to harmful substances like asbestos, radon, or radiation can increase cancer risk.
  • Genetic Factors: A family history of cancer can increase your risk. Genetic testing can help identify specific hereditary risks.
  • Age: The risk of developing cancer increases with age, with most cases occurring in people over 50.

Cancer Prevention

While not all cancers can be prevented, you can reduce your risk by:

  • Avoiding Tobacco: Smoking is linked to several types of cancer. Quitting smoking at any age can significantly lower your risk.
  • Maintaining a Healthy Diet: Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains. Limit red and processed meats.
  • Staying Physically Active: Regular exercise helps maintain a healthy weight and reduces the risk of several types of cancer.
  • Protecting Yourself from the Sun: Use sunscreen, wear protective clothing, and avoid tanning beds to reduce the risk of skin cancer.
  • Getting Vaccinated: Vaccinations like the HPV vaccine can prevent infections that may lead to cancer.

Treatment Options

Cancer treatment varies based on the type, stage, and individual patient factors. Common treatment methods include:

  • Surgery: Removing the tumor or cancerous tissue.
  • Radiation Therapy: Using high-energy radiation to kill or shrink cancer cells.
  • Chemotherapy: Using drugs to kill cancer cells throughout the body.
  • Immunotherapy: Boosting the body’s immune system to fight cancer.
  • Targeted Therapy: Using drugs that target specific molecules involved in cancer growth.
  • Hormone Therapy: For cancers that are driven by hormones, like some breast and prostate cancers.

Living with Cancer

Receiving a cancer diagnosis can be overwhelming, but there are many resources available to help manage the emotional, physical, and practical aspects of the disease. Support groups, counseling, and palliative care can improve quality of life for patients and their families.
